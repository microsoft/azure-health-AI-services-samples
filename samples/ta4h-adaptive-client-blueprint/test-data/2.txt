HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS: The patient has been experiencing cough and shortness of breath for the past several months. She has been evaluated by her primary care physician and treated for presumed bronchitis without improvement. She has also tried several over-the-counter medications without relief. She has no significant medical history, no recent travel, no known exposures to tuberculosis or other respiratory illnesses. She denies fever, chills, night sweats, weight loss or hemoptysis.

REVIEW OF SYSTEMS: The patient complains of cough and shortness of breath with minimal exertion. She denies chest pain, palpitations or syncope. She has no abdominal or urinary symptoms. She denies any skin rashes or joint pain.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION: Vital signs are within normal limits. Oxygen saturation is 94% on room air. Lungs are clear to auscultation. No wheezing, rales or rhonchi are heard. Cardiac examination is within normal limits. Abdomen is soft, non-tender with no hepatosplenomegaly. No peripheral edema.

LABORATORY WORK: CBC, CMP, TSH, BNP and ESR are within normal limits. Tuberculosis skin test is negative.

IMPRESSION AND PLAN: The patient's symptoms are concerning for a possible pulmonary pathology. I recommend a chest x-ray, pulmonary function tests and high resolution CT scan of the chest. Further management will depend on the results of these tests.```
